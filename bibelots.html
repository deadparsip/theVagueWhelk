Vague whelk, look not upon my foul form

News is an abritrary collection of upsetting and troubling ideas that sometimes seem to be connected to real-world events but nearly always make you feel dry and alone like the last jaffa cake in Hull.

Puttymo

Given that over forty and a one months have passed since the Chalk Farm fiasco, I have recently felt the compulsion and possibly the desire, and maybe even the urge if you will, to look into maybe taking up some new form of semi-employment.
As those of you know me well will know quite well or even weller, I am a man who likes to keep busy, and must at all times be using my hands for constructive purposes. Bad things have happened when I have become distracted or bored. For example I once knitted a badger to its own mind over a particularly quiet bank holiday weekend.
I have spent over twoty or threety years and thirty emblems at reed; it was there I first learnt to communicate solely with my eyebrows, how to make socks from road kill, and how to become employed through a care in the community program without anyone actually finding out about my uni-bottocked situation or my brain spoon.
For these reasons I have become quite attached to the place and thus it was quite a difficult decision to actually leave. I felt like a very handsome blister being suddenly ripped from its host.
What actually snipped the final hair on my lumpy back parts, in the end, was my passing interest in Putty. I have always been fascinated by any substance that can be soft one minute, and then reasonably hard only a few hours later (given the right temperature and humidity conditions).
So far, my time at Puttymo has been reasonably successful: my hands have been kept busy for the majority of the time, my brain also, and there are even chocolate bourbons for those of a will to eat chocolate bourbons. Of which I count myself one. Of. Or sometimes 1.2 when I have eaten a large number of chocolate bourbons. Which is most days when there aren't custard creams. Cremes?. Only time will tell if I am accepted - one can usually expect the withering-away-and-dying stage to commence within the first 6 months if I am not accepted by the host. There is quite a lot of me to wither so I am confident I can escape if I need to.

Farming chalk

Yesterday lifetime, I was offered a position, namely that of chalk farmer. Chalk supplies in London are extremely low at the moment, due largely to a recent growth in natural blackboard predators, apparently.
As a journalist/badger farmer/web developerer and table, this seemed to me like a natural career progression and in any case, I really really do like chalk, so I accepted immediately.
Interestingly, it was at a small to medium to large sized farm in the Chalk Farm area. This is just a few hundred miles north of Sydenham for those who aren't familiar.
I have always wanted to farm chalk on a chalk farm in Chalk Farm. I have to admit, this has long been a dream of mine.
But, dear feet, I was concerned. I was concerned that should anyone ask me what I did for a living, it would be difficult to guarantee that it would come out coherently every time. There might be occasions where I slur a little or I say "at chalk farm" instead of "on chalk farm". Or people might just think I have a stutter. They wouldn't necessarily UNDERSTAND.
Obviously, as any fule know, there is obvious danger here. It would in all liklihood lead to complete and utter chaos and maybe even death. I am not by my nature a man much inclined to chaos. I prefer something in the middle of chaos and order, leaning slightly to the chaos side of things. Think of a TK Max or something. But not as bad. Maybe a MacDonalds on a Saturday morning.
With less large multi-offsprung women in tracksuits. Slightly less. So, if you are reading this, Chalk Farm Chalk Farmers Recruitment inc, Chalk Farm, I am sure you will understand. I CANNOT TOLERATE CHAOS.

So I killed myself and set fire to the body fourteen times, to make sure no one found the evidence.

Creamy Opening

Can you test that all of our custard is thick enough to meet our high standards?

By that I mean you should not be able to swim in it even if you are really really strong, like a carbon fiber whiskey.

When I was a boy, I often dreamt of working in a custard factory. Imagine, I thought to myself during one of my wetter childish reveries, imagine if I could actually walk about in a place that makes my favourite yellow non-newtonian liquid-based dessert. I would probably either die of happy, or just be quite pleased for a while until I realised it was a fruitless* dream and then moved on to the trifle factory down the road. (ha)

Either way, Imagine! I reveried. I reveried hard. Harder than anyone else of that era, to my knowledge.
Today, that dream has come true. And even though you should never start a sentence with And, I want to share that never ending joy with you, an unemployed custard-ignoring fiend.

Imagine!
